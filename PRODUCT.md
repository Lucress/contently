# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

**Primary**: Solo creator-entrepreneurs — YouTubers, TikTokers, and podcasters who monetise through brand sponsorships and partnerships. They're running a one-person media business: they plan content, film it, and also chase deals, send invoices, and track revenue. Their context switches between creative work and business work within the same day.

**Surface split**: The app dashboard is the product surface (ideas, pipeline, brand CRM, revenue, email hub). There is also a public-facing brand surface (landing page, blog) aimed at prospective creators evaluating the tool; that surface calls for the same personality but a brand register.

## Product Purpose

Contently is the operating system for a creator's business. It covers the full lifecycle — from first spark of an idea through scripting, filming, editing, scheduling, and publishing — while also handling the business layer: brand relationships, deal tracking, revenue logging, and analytics. It exists so creators never have to cobble together Notion, a spreadsheet, and a Gmail inbox to run their work.

## Positioning

The only tool built for the full creator lifecycle — content pipeline and brand business, in one place.

## Brand Personality

Creative, warm, and encouraging. The app should feel like a capable creative partner, not a project manager. It is confident and polished enough that a creator wouldn't hesitate to screenshot a dashboard and send it to a brand contact — but it never feels stiff or corporate. Voice is direct, human, and quietly enthusiastic about the work creators do.

## Anti-references

**Corporate SaaS**: Not Salesforce, not HubSpot, not any tool that feels like it was designed for a procurement team. No cold blues, no dense data tables without personality, no enterprise-speak in copy.

**Childish or toy-like**: Not Canva's bubbly loudness. Warm does not mean playful-to-the-point-of-unserious. The product is used in business conversations with brand partners; it must remain credible.

## Design Principles

**Creative partner, not a tracker.** Every interaction — empty states, transitions, microcopy, status labels — should feel like the app is on the creator's side, not auditing them.

**Credible at a glance.** A creator may share a screenshot with a brand partner. The UI must look polished and professional without being cold. Warm ≠ amateurish.

**Pipeline as the organising metaphor.** The idea-to-published journey is the spine. Every screen should make the creator's next action obvious — what to do now, what's coming up, what's done.

**Business is first-class.** Revenue, brand CRM, and analytics are not bolt-ons. They sit at the same level as content creation tools, because for this user they are equally important.

**Warm without being childish.** Colour, roundness, and personality are welcome. Excess decoration, cartoon-adjacent iconography, or anything that undercuts professional credibility is not.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA minimum. Keyboard navigation for all interactive elements. Sufficient contrast for text and interactive states. Reduced-motion support for animations (Framer Motion variants should respect `prefers-reduced-motion`).
